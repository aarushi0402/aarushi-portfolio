Product Thinking

How I Approach Building

I'm increasingly drawn to thinking beyond just code — toward understanding why something should be built in the first place. As I work on AI/ML projects, I've started paying more attention to the user side of what I build — what problem it solves, who it helps, and whether it actually makes a difference.

🔍
Starting with the User
I try to think about the person using the product — their goals, frustrations, and context — before jumping into solutions.
📐
Defining the Problem Clearly
I'm learning that building the right thing matters more than building something complex. I focus on breaking problems down before solving them.
📊
Thinking in Terms of Outcomes
With my technical background, I'm comfortable working with data — and I'm beginning to think more in terms of what success looks like (engagement, usefulness, accuracy), not just implementation.
🔁
Building and Iterating
Through my projects, I've started to treat each version as something to improve — not just complete. I value feedback and iteration over perfection.
🤝
Bridging Tech and Users
I enjoy being at the intersection of engineering and user experience — where technical decisions directly impact how people interact with a product.
🌱
Currently Exploring
How product decisions are made, basic product frameworks and prioritisation methods, user research and feedback collection, and how AI can be integrated meaningfully into real products.

My direction: I want to grow into roles where I can combine AI/ML with product thinking — building systems that are not just technically strong, but also useful, usable, and impactful.
